About Adalaj Ni Vav

Adalaj Ni Vav is a stunning five-story stepwell located in Gandhinagar, Gujarat Built in 1498 by Queen Rudabai, this architectural marvel served as both a water reservoir and a resting place for travelers.

The stepwell is renowned for its intricate carvings, ornate pillars, and mythological motifs, The walls depict Hindu, Jain, and Islamic influences, making it a fine example of Indo-Islamic architecture.

Unlike traditional stepwells, Adalaj Ni Vav has ventilation openings that keep the interiors cool even during peak summers, providing a peaceful retreat for visitors.

Today, the site is a major tourist attraction admired for its historical significance and craftsmanship.
