About Rani Ki Vav

Rani Ki Vav, located in Patan, Gujarat, is an UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most intricately designed stepwells in India. Built by Queen Udayamati in the 11th century this stepwell is an architectural masterpiece showcasing beautiful carvings and sculptures.

The stepwell is over seven stories deep and features sculptures of Hindu deities, apsaras, and intricate motifs. It was constructed as a water storage system and represents the rich artistic heritage of the Solanki dynasty.
